<p><p>The offshore wind turbine market features various types based on installation techniques. Mono pile structures are single, large cylindrical foundations driven into the seabed, ideal for shallow waters. Jacket foundations consist of a lattice design providing stability in deeper waters. Tripod foundations include three legs for enhanced support, suitable for moderate depths. Floating turbines are anchored to the seabed with flexible moorings, allowing deployment in deep water, maximizing energy generation potential where traditional methods are impractical.</p></p>

<p><p>The offshore wind turbines market is categorized by water depth: shallow water, transitional water, and deep water. Deep water turbines, beyond 200 meters, often employ advanced floating technology, enabling harnessing of stronger winds further offshore, thereby increasing energy generation potential.</p></p>
