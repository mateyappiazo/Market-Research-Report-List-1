<p><p>Semiconductor wafer electrostatic chucks (ESC) are essential components used in semiconductor manufacturing for holding wafers in place during processes like etching and deposition. The market for ESCs features two primary types: Coulomb type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type. Coulomb type ESCs utilize electrostatic forces to secure wafers, offering robust adhesion and stability. In contrast, JR type ESCs employ a combination of electrostatic forces and vacuum for improved performance and flexibility, catering to diverse manufacturing requirements and enhancing process efficiency.</p></p>
