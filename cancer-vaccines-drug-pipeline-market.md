<p><p>The cancer vaccine drug pipeline is divided into two main market types: therapeutic and prophylactic vaccines. Therapeutic vaccines aim to treat existing cancer by stimulating the immune system to recognize and attack cancer cells, often tailored to individual patients. In contrast, prophylactic vaccines are designed to prevent cancer from developing by eliciting an immune response against specific cancer-causing agents, like certain viruses. Both types play crucial roles in advancing cancer treatment and prevention strategies within the biotechnology landscape.</p></p>
