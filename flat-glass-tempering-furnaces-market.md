<p><p>The flat glass tempering furnace market consists of two primary types: horizontal and vertical. Horizontal furnaces are designed for continuous processing, allowing sheets of glass to pass through in a flat orientation, which optimizes production efficiency. In contrast, vertical furnaces accommodate glass in an upright position, making them suitable for thicker or larger glass panes. Both types cater to diverse industry needs, with horizontal models favored for high-volume operations, while vertical units offer flexibility for custom glass products and specialized applications.</p></p>
